# Gross Anatomy - Procedures

## Overview

Anatomical procedures encompass diagnostic, therapeutic, and surgical interventions that require detailed knowledge of human anatomy. These procedures range from basic clinical examinations to complex surgical operations, all requiring precise understanding of anatomical relationships, surface landmarks, and tissue planes.

### Categories of Procedures
- **Diagnostic procedures**: Physical examination, imaging-guided procedures
- **Minimally invasive procedures**: Injections, biopsies, catheterizations
- **Surgical procedures**: Open and laparoscopic operations
- **Emergency procedures**: Trauma interventions, life-saving procedures

## Physical Examination Procedures

### Systematic Physical Examination

#### General Inspection
- **Overall appearance**: Posture, gait, distress level
- **Vital signs**: Blood pressure, pulse, respirations, temperature
- **Anthropometric measurements**: Height, weight, BMI
- **Skin examination**: Color, texture, lesions, hydration

#### Head and Neck Examination
- **Cranial inspection**: Symmetry, deformities, masses
- **Palpation**: Temporal arteries, lymph nodes, thyroid
- **HEENT examination**: Eyes, ears, nose, throat assessment
- **Neurological**: Cranial nerve testing

#### Chest Examination
- **Inspection**: Respiratory pattern, chest wall movement
- **Palpation**: Chest expansion, tactile fremitus
- **Percussion**: Lung resonance, cardiac borders
- **Auscultation**: Breath sounds, heart sounds

#### Abdominal Examination
- **Inspection**: Contour, visible peristalsis, masses
- **Auscultation**: Bowel sounds, bruits (before palpation)
- **Palpation**: Light and deep, organ assessment
- **Percussion**: Liver span, spleen size, ascites

#### Extremity Examination
- **Inspection**: Deformities, swelling, skin changes
- **Palpation**: Pulses, temperature, tenderness
- **Range of motion**: Active and passive movement
- **Neurological**: Reflexes, sensation, strength

### Specialized Examination Techniques

#### Cardiovascular Assessment
- **Point of maximal impulse**: Left ventricular apex location
- **Jugular venous pressure**: Central venous pressure estimation
- **Peripheral pulse examination**: All major pulse points
- **Ankle-brachial index**: Peripheral vascular disease screening

#### Respiratory Assessment
- **Chest expansion measurement**: Respiratory function
- **Diaphragmatic excursion**: Percussion technique
- **Fremitus assessment**: Vocal and tactile fremitus
- **Peak flow measurement**: Airway function

#### Neurological Assessment
- **Mental status examination**: Cognitive function
- **Motor examination**: Strength, tone, coordination
- **Sensory examination**: All sensory modalities
- **Reflex testing**: Deep tendon and pathological reflexes

## Injection Procedures

### Intramuscular Injections

#### Deltoid Injection
- **Anatomy**: Deltoid muscle, axillary nerve location
- **Landmarks**: Acromion process, deltoid tuberosity
- **Technique**: Anterolateral approach, 1-inch needle
- **Volume limit**: 1-2 mL maximum

#### Gluteal Injections
- **Ventrogluteal site**: Preferred for adults
- **Landmarks**: Anterior superior iliac spine, iliac crest
- **Anatomy**: Gluteus medius muscle
- **Safety**: Avoid sciatic nerve, superior gluteal vessels

#### Vastus Lateralis
- **Anatomy**: Lateral thigh muscle
- **Landmarks**: Greater trochanter to lateral femoral condyle
- **Preferred site**: Infants and children
- **Volume**: Up to 5 mL in adults

### Intravenous Access

#### Peripheral IV Placement
- **Preferred sites**: Dorsal hand veins, forearm cephalic/basilic
- **Anatomy**: Superficial venous system
- **Technique**: Catheter-over-needle, aseptic technique
- **Complications**: Infiltration, phlebitis, infection

#### Central Venous Access
- **Internal jugular approach**: Triangle of safety
- **Subclavian approach**: Infraclavicular insertion
- **Femoral approach**: Below inguinal ligament
- **Ultrasound guidance**: Reduces complications

### Joint Injections

#### Knee Joint (Intra-articular)
- **Anatomy**: Suprapatellar pouch, joint capsule
- **Approaches**: Lateral, medial, anterior
- **Indications**: Arthritis, bursitis, diagnostic sampling
- **Complications**: Infection, bleeding, nerve injury

#### Shoulder Joint
- **Anatomy**: Glenohumeral joint space
- **Approaches**: Posterior, anterior, lateral
- **Landmarks**: Acromion, coracoid process
- **Ultrasound guidance**: Improves accuracy

## Diagnostic Procedures

### Biopsy Procedures

#### Skin Biopsy
- **Types**: Punch, shave, excisional
- **Anatomy**: Epidermis, dermis, subcutaneous tissue
- **Technique**: Local anesthesia, sterile procedure
- **Wound care**: Hemostasis, dressing, suture removal

#### Lymph Node Biopsy
- **Fine needle aspiration**: Cytological sampling
- **Core needle biopsy**: Histological architecture
- **Surgical excision**: Complete node removal
- **Anatomical considerations**: Nearby vital structures

#### Bone Marrow Biopsy
- **Sites**: Posterior iliac crest (preferred), sternum
- **Anatomy**: Cortical and trabecular bone
- **Technique**: Aspiration and core biopsy
- **Complications**: Pain, bleeding, infection

### Lumbar Puncture
- **Anatomy**: Subarachnoid space, conus medullaris level
- **Landmarks**: L3-L4 or L4-L5 interspace
- **Technique**: Lateral decubitus or sitting position
- **CSF analysis**: Pressure, cell count, chemistry, culture

### Thoracentesis
- **Anatomy**: Pleural space, neurovascular bundle
- **Landmarks**: Posterior axillary line, below effusion level
- **Technique**: Over rib superior border, Z-track method
- **Complications**: Pneumothorax, bleeding, infection

### Paracentesis
- **Anatomy**: Peritoneal cavity, inferior epigastric vessels
- **Landmarks**: Left lower quadrant, lateral to rectus
- **Technique**: Z-track, incremental advancement
- **Analysis**: Cell count, culture, chemistry, cytology

## Catheterization Procedures

### Urinary Catheterization

#### Male Catheterization
- **Anatomy**: Penile urethra, prostatic urethra
- **Technique**: Sterile technique, lubricant, gentle pressure
- **Obstacles**: Prostatic enlargement, urethral stricture
- **Balloon inflation**: Confirm bladder position

#### Female Catheterization
- **Anatomy**: Short, straight urethra
- **Landmarks**: Urethral meatus, vaginal introitus
- **Technique**: Anterior to vaginal opening
- **Complications**: UTI, urethral trauma

### Nasogastric Tube Insertion
- **Anatomy**: Nasal cavity, pharynx, esophagus, stomach
- **Technique**: Lubrication, flexed neck, swallowing
- **Confirmation**: X-ray, pH testing, aspiration
- **Complications**: Esophageal perforation, pneumothorax

## Surgical Procedures

### Basic Surgical Principles

#### Surgical Anatomy
- **Tissue planes**: Fascial layers, anatomical spaces
- **Neurovascular bundles**: Critical structure identification
- **Surgical approaches**: Minimize tissue damage
- **Anatomical landmarks**: Consistent reference points

#### Incision Placement
- **Skin tension lines**: Langer's lines for optimal healing
- **Cosmetic considerations**: Hidden in natural creases
- **Functional considerations**: Avoid nerve and vessel injury
- **Access requirements**: Adequate exposure

### Common Surgical Procedures

#### Appendectomy
- **Anatomy**: Appendix location, cecum, mesoappendix
- **Approaches**: Open (McBurney's), laparoscopic
- **Landmarks**: McBurney's point, right iliac fossa
- **Complications**: Perforation, abscess, wound infection

#### Cholecystectomy
- **Anatomy**: Gallbladder, Calot's triangle, liver bed
- **Critical view**: Hepatocystic triangle visualization
- **Laparoscopic technique**: Four-port standard approach
- **Complications**: Bile duct injury, bleeding

#### Hernia Repair
- **Inguinal hernia anatomy**: Inguinal canal, hesselbach triangle
- **Types**: Direct vs indirect inguinal hernias
- **Repair techniques**: Tension-free mesh repair
- **Laparoscopic approach**: TEP, TAPP procedures

#### Thyroidectomy
- **Anatomy**: Thyroid gland, recurrent laryngeal nerve
- **Critical structures**: Parathyroid glands, vessels
- **Technique**: Capsular dissection, nerve monitoring
- **Complications**: Voice changes, hypocalcemia

## Emergency Procedures

### Airway Management

#### Endotracheal Intubation
- **Anatomy**: Larynx, vocal cords, trachea
- **Landmarks**: Epiglottis, arytenoid cartilages
- **Technique**: Direct laryngoscopy, video laryngoscopy
- **Confirmation**: Capnography, chest rise, breath sounds

#### Cricothyrotomy
- **Anatomy**: Cricothyroid membrane, thyroid cartilage
- **Indications**: Cannot intubate, cannot ventilate
- **Technique**: Palpation, horizontal incision, tube insertion
- **Complications**: Bleeding, esophageal perforation

### Chest Procedures

#### Chest Tube Insertion
- **Anatomy**: Pleural space, intercostal neurovascular bundle
- **Site**: 5th intercostal space, anterior axillary line
- **Technique**: Blunt dissection, finger sweep
- **Complications**: Lung injury, bleeding, malposition

#### Pericardiocentesis
- **Anatomy**: Pericardial space, cardiac chambers
- **Approach**: Subxiphoid, parasternal
- **Technique**: ECG monitoring, imaging guidance
- **Complications**: Cardiac perforation, arrhythmias

### Vascular Access

#### Arterial Cannulation
- **Sites**: Radial, femoral, brachial arteries
- **Anatomy**: Arterial pulsation, collateral circulation
- **Technique**: Seldinger technique, ultrasound guidance
- **Monitoring**: Blood pressure, arterial blood gases

#### Intraosseous Access
- **Sites**: Proximal tibia, distal femur, proximal humerus
- **Anatomy**: Cortical bone, marrow cavity
- **Indications**: Failed peripheral access in emergency
- **Technique**: Power drill, manual insertion

## Imaging-Guided Procedures

### Ultrasound-Guided Procedures
- **Vascular access**: Central lines, peripheral IVs
- **Biopsies**: Liver, kidney, thyroid, lymph nodes
- **Joint injections**: Real-time needle guidance
- **Thoracentesis**: Pleural effusion drainage

### CT-Guided Procedures
- **Lung biopsy**: Peripheral lung lesions
- **Abscess drainage**: Percutaneous approach
- **Spine procedures**: Facet injections, biopsies
- **Tumor ablation**: Radiofrequency, cryotherapy

### Fluoroscopy-Guided Procedures
- **Angiography**: Vascular imaging and intervention
- **GI procedures**: Contrast studies, stent placement
- **Orthopedic procedures**: Fracture reduction, hardware placement
- **Pain management**: Epidural injections, nerve blocks

## Complications and Safety

### Common Complications
- **Bleeding**: Vascular injury, coagulopathy
- **Infection**: Sterile technique violations
- **Nerve injury**: Anatomical variant, technique error
- **Organ perforation**: Excessive force, poor visualization

### Risk Reduction Strategies
- **Anatomical knowledge**: Understanding normal and variant anatomy
- **Proper technique**: Standardized approaches
- **Imaging guidance**: Real-time visualization
- **Sterile technique**: Infection prevention

### Emergency Management
- **Recognition**: Early identification of complications
- **Immediate action**: Stop procedure, direct pressure
- **Resuscitation**: IV access, fluid replacement
- **Surgical consultation**: Complex complications

## Key Points

- Anatomical procedures require detailed knowledge of human anatomy
- Physical examination is the foundation of clinical assessment
- Injection techniques must consider anatomical landmarks and safety
- Biopsy procedures provide tissue diagnosis with minimal morbidity
- Catheterization procedures require sterile technique and anatomical knowledge
- Surgical procedures follow anatomical planes and preserve vital structures
- Emergency procedures save lives but carry inherent risks
- Imaging guidance improves procedure safety and accuracy
- Complication prevention requires anatomical expertise and proper technique
- Continuous education and practice maintain procedural competency
