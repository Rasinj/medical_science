# Gross Anatomy - Overview

## Overview

Gross anatomy is the study of anatomical structures visible to the naked eye, without the aid of magnification. It provides the foundation for understanding human body organization, structure-function relationships, and clinical correlations essential for medical practice.

### Definition and Scope
- **Gross anatomy**: Study of structures visible without microscopy
- **Systematic anatomy**: Study by organ systems
- **Regional anatomy**: Study by body regions
- **Clinical anatomy**: Application to medical practice

## Anatomical Organization

### Levels of Organization
1. **Chemical level**: Atoms and molecules
2. **Cellular level**: Basic unit of life
3. **Tissue level**: Groups of similar cells
4. **Organ level**: Structures with specific functions
5. **System level**: Groups of organs working together
6. **Organism level**: Complete living individual

### Body Systems

#### Musculoskeletal System
- **Skeletal system**: 206 bones, joints, cartilage
- **Muscular system**: 600+ muscles for movement
- **Functions**: Support, protection, movement, mineral storage
- **Components**: Axial and appendicular skeleton

#### Cardiovascular System
- **Heart**: Four-chambered pump
- **Blood vessels**: Arteries, veins, capillaries
- **Functions**: Transport of nutrients, gases, waste
- **Circulation**: Pulmonary and systemic circuits

#### Respiratory System
- **Upper respiratory tract**: Nose, pharynx, larynx
- **Lower respiratory tract**: Trachea, bronchi, lungs
- **Functions**: Gas exchange, pH regulation
- **Mechanics**: Ventilation and respiration

#### Nervous System
- **Central nervous system**: Brain and spinal cord
- **Peripheral nervous system**: Nerves and ganglia
- **Functions**: Control, coordination, communication
- **Divisions**: Somatic and autonomic systems

#### Endocrine System
- **Glands**: Hormone-producing organs
- **Functions**: Chemical regulation and communication
- **Major glands**: Pituitary, thyroid, adrenals, pancreas
- **Integration**: Works with nervous system

#### Digestive System
- **Alimentary canal**: GI tract from mouth to anus
- **Accessory organs**: Liver, gallbladder, pancreas
- **Functions**: Digestion, absorption, elimination
- **Layers**: Mucosa, submucosa, muscularis, serosa

#### Urinary System
- **Kidneys**: Filter blood, produce urine
- **Ureters, bladder, urethra**: Transport and store urine
- **Functions**: Excretion, fluid balance, pH regulation
- **Nephron**: Functional unit of kidney

#### Reproductive System
- **Male**: Testes, ducts, accessory glands, penis
- **Female**: Ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus, vagina
- **Functions**: Reproduction, hormone production
- **Development**: Secondary sexual characteristics

#### Integumentary System
- **Skin**: Largest organ of the body
- **Appendages**: Hair, nails, glands
- **Functions**: Protection, thermoregulation, sensation
- **Layers**: Epidermis, dermis, hypodermis

#### Lymphatic and Immune System
- **Lymphatic vessels**: Parallel circulation system
- **Lymphoid organs**: Lymph nodes, spleen, thymus
- **Functions**: Immunity, fluid balance
- **Components**: Lymphocytes, antibodies, complement

## Anatomical Position and Terminology

### Standard Anatomical Position
- **Upright standing**: Feet together, facing forward
- **Arms at sides**: Palms facing forward
- **Head level**: Eyes looking straight ahead
- **Purpose**: Universal reference point

### Directional Terms
- **Superior/Inferior**: Above/below
- **Anterior/Posterior**: Front/back
- **Medial/Lateral**: Toward/away from midline
- **Proximal/Distal**: Near/far from attachment point
- **Superficial/Deep**: Near/far from surface

### Planes and Sections
- **Sagittal plane**: Divides into left and right
- **Frontal (coronal) plane**: Divides into anterior and posterior
- **Transverse (horizontal) plane**: Divides into superior and inferior
- **Oblique plane**: Diagonal sections

### Body Cavities

#### Dorsal Cavity
- **Cranial cavity**: Contains brain
- **Vertebral cavity**: Contains spinal cord
- **Protection**: Bony encasement

#### Ventral Cavity
- **Thoracic cavity**: Heart, lungs, major vessels
- **Abdominopelvic cavity**: Digestive, urinary, reproductive organs
- **Diaphragm**: Separates thoracic and abdominal cavities

#### Membrane-Lined Cavities
- **Pleural cavities**: Surround lungs
- **Pericardial cavity**: Surrounds heart
- **Peritoneal cavity**: Abdominal organs

## Regional Anatomy

### Head and Neck
- **Skull**: Cranium and facial bones
- **Brain**: Cerebrum, cerebellum, brainstem
- **Special senses**: Eyes, ears, nose, tongue
- **Neck structures**: Cervical vertebrae, major vessels

### Thorax
- **Thoracic wall**: Ribs, sternum, muscles
- **Mediastinum**: Central thoracic compartment
- **Lungs**: Right (3 lobes), left (2 lobes)
- **Heart**: Four chambers, major vessels

### Abdomen
- **Abdominal wall**: Muscles, fascia, skin
- **Digestive organs**: Stomach, intestines, liver
- **Retroperitoneum**: Kidneys, major vessels
- **Regions**: 9 regions or 4 quadrants

### Pelvis
- **Pelvic girdle**: Hip bones, sacrum, coccyx
- **Pelvic organs**: Bladder, reproductive organs
- **Pelvic floor**: Supportive muscle layer
- **Sex differences**: Male vs female pelvis

### Upper Extremity
- **Shoulder**: Clavicle, scapula, humerus
- **Arm**: Humerus and associated muscles
- **Forearm**: Radius, ulna, muscles
- **Hand**: Carpals, metacarpals, phalanges

### Lower Extremity
- **Hip**: Pelvis, femur, joint structures
- **Thigh**: Femur and muscle compartments
- **Leg**: Tibia, fibula, muscles
- **Foot**: Tarsals, metatarsals, phalanges
