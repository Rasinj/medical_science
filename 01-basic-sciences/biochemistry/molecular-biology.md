# Molecular Biology

## Overview

Molecular biology is the study of biological processes at the molecular level, focusing on the structure and function of macromolecules essential to life. It encompasses DNA, RNA, and protein interactions that govern cellular function, inheritance, and regulation of gene expression.

### Central Dogma
**DNA → RNA → Protein**
- **Replication**: DNA copies itself for cell division
- **Transcription**: DNA information transferred to RNA
- **Translation**: RNA information converted to proteins
- **Reverse transcription**: Some RNA viruses can synthesize DNA

## DNA Structure and Function

### DNA Structure
- **Double helix**: Two antiparallel strands wound around common axis
- **Base pairing**: A-T (2 hydrogen bonds), G-C (3 hydrogen bonds)
- **Major groove**: Site for protein-DNA interactions
- **Minor groove**: Additional protein binding sites

### DNA Components
- **Nucleotides**: Phosphate + ribose + nitrogenous base
- **Bases**: Purines (A, G) and pyrimidines (C, T)
- **Backbone**: Phosphodiester bonds link 3' and 5' carbons
- **Directionality**: 5' to 3' direction of synthesis

### DNA Packaging
- **Histones**: Basic proteins around which DNA wraps
- **Nucleosomes**: DNA wrapped around histone octamers
- **Chromatin**: Nucleosomes and associated proteins
- **Chromosomes**: Highly condensed chromatin during cell division

## DNA Replication

### Replication Process
1. **Initiation**: Origin recognition, helicase unwinds DNA
2. **Elongation**: DNA polymerase synthesizes new strands
3. **Termination**: Completion and processing of new DNA

### Key Enzymes
- **Helicase**: Unwinds double helix
- **Primase**: Synthesizes RNA primers
- **DNA polymerase III**: Main replicative enzyme (prokaryotes)
- **DNA polymerase α, δ, ε**: Eukaryotic replicative enzymes
- **Ligase**: Joins Okazaki fragments

### Replication Features
- **Semiconservative**: Each new DNA molecule contains one original strand
- **Bidirectional**: Proceeds in both directions from origin
- **Leading strand**: Continuous synthesis (5' to 3')
- **Lagging strand**: Discontinuous synthesis (Okazaki fragments)

## RNA Structure and Types

### RNA Structure
- **Single-stranded**: Unlike DNA's double helix
- **Ribose sugar**: Contains 2'-OH group
- **Uracil**: Replaces thymine in RNA
- **Secondary structure**: Base pairing creates hairpins, loops

### Types of RNA
1. **Messenger RNA (mRNA)**: Carries genetic information for protein synthesis
2. **Transfer RNA (tRNA)**: Delivers amino acids to ribosomes
3. **Ribosomal RNA (rRNA)**: Structural and catalytic component of ribosomes
4. **MicroRNA (miRNA)**: Regulates gene expression post-transcriptionally
5. **Long non-coding RNA (lncRNA)**: Various regulatory functions

## Transcription

### Transcription Process
1. **Initiation**: RNA polymerase binds promoter, begins synthesis
2. **Elongation**: RNA polymerase moves along DNA template
3. **Termination**: Transcription ends at terminator sequences

### Prokaryotic Transcription
- **RNA polymerase**: Single enzyme for all genes
- **Promoter**: -10 and -35 consensus sequences
- **Sigma factors**: Direct RNA polymerase to specific promoters
- **Operons**: Clustered genes with shared regulation

### Eukaryotic Transcription
- **RNA polymerase I**: rRNA genes (except 5S)
- **RNA polymerase II**: mRNA and most non-coding RNAs
- **RNA polymerase III**: tRNA and 5S rRNA genes
- **Transcription factors**: Required for initiation

### RNA Processing (Eukaryotes)
1. **5' capping**: 7-methylguanosine cap addition
2. **3' polyadenylation**: Poly-A tail addition
3. **Splicing**: Intron removal, exon joining
4. **Alternative splicing**: Different combinations of exons

## Translation

### Translation Process
1. **Initiation**: Ribosome assembles at start codon (AUG)
2. **Elongation**: Amino acids added according to mRNA codons
3. **Termination**: Release at stop codons (UAA, UAG, UGA)

### Genetic Code
- **Triplet code**: Three nucleotides specify one amino acid
- **Degeneracy**: Multiple codons for same amino acid
- **Universality**: Same code used by most organisms
- **Non-overlapping**: Each nucleotide belongs to one codon

### Ribosome Structure
- **Large subunit**: Peptidyl transferase center (catalytic)
- **Small subunit**: Decoding center (mRNA binding)
- **A site**: Aminoacyl-tRNA binding
- **P site**: Peptidyl-tRNA binding
- **E site**: Exit site for deacylated tRNA

### Translation Control
- **Cap-dependent initiation**: Standard mechanism
- **IRES**: Internal ribosome entry sites (cap-independent)
- **miRNA**: Post-transcriptional regulation
- **Ribosome profiling**: Maps translation globally

## Gene Regulation

### Transcriptional Control
- **Promoters**: Core elements for transcription initiation
- **Enhancers**: Distant elements that increase transcription
- **Silencers**: Elements that decrease transcription
- **Transcription factors**: Proteins that regulate transcription

### Epigenetic Regulation
- **DNA methylation**: CpG methylation silences genes
- **Histone modifications**: Acetylation, methylation affect transcription
- **Chromatin remodeling**: ATP-dependent nucleosome movement
- **Non-coding RNAs**: Regulatory RNAs control gene expression

### Post-transcriptional Control
- **Alternative splicing**: Tissue-specific isoforms
- **miRNA regulation**: Target mRNA degradation or translation inhibition
- **RNA binding proteins**: Affect mRNA stability and translation
- **Nonsense-mediated decay**: Quality control mechanism

## DNA Repair Mechanisms

### Types of DNA Damage
- **Spontaneous**: Depurination, deamination, oxidation
- **Induced**: UV radiation, chemical mutagens, ionizing radiation
- **Replication errors**: Mismatches, slippage

### Repair Pathways
1. **Base excision repair (BER)**: Single base damage
2. **Nucleotide excision repair (NER)**: Bulky lesions
3. **Mismatch repair (MMR)**: Replication errors
4. **Homologous recombination**: Double-strand breaks
5. **Non-homologous end joining**: Double-strand break repair

## Cell Cycle and DNA

### Cell Cycle Phases
- **G1**: Gap 1, cell growth
- **S**: Synthesis, DNA replication
- **G2**: Gap 2, preparation for mitosis
- **M**: Mitosis, cell division
- **G0**: Quiescent state

### Cell Cycle Checkpoints
- **G1/S checkpoint**: DNA damage detection
- **Intra-S checkpoint**: Replication fork problems
- **G2/M checkpoint**: DNA replication completion
- **Spindle checkpoint**: Chromosome attachment

## Clinical Applications

### Molecular Diagnostics
- **PCR**: Amplification of specific DNA sequences
- **Real-time PCR**: Quantitative DNA/RNA analysis
- **DNA sequencing**: Determining nucleotide sequences
- **Microarrays**: Gene expression profiling
- **RNA-seq**: Comprehensive transcriptome analysis

### Gene Therapy
- **Viral vectors**: Modified viruses deliver therapeutic genes
- **Non-viral methods**: Lipofection, electroporation
- **CRISPR/Cas9**: Precise genome editing
- **Antisense oligonucleotides**: Modulate gene expression

### Personalized Medicine
- **Pharmacogenomics**: Drug response based on genetics
- **Cancer genomics**: Mutation profiling for therapy selection
- **Genetic testing**: Disease susceptibility assessment
- **Biomarkers**: Molecular indicators of disease state

### Common Genetic Disorders
- **Single gene disorders**: Cystic fibrosis, sickle cell disease
- **Chromosomal disorders**: Down syndrome, Turner syndrome
- **Multifactorial disorders**: Diabetes, heart disease
- **Cancer**: Oncogenes and tumor suppressor mutations

## Molecular Techniques

### DNA Analysis
- **Restriction enzymes**: Cut DNA at specific sequences
- **Gel electrophoresis**: Separate DNA by size
- **Southern blotting**: Detect specific DNA sequences
- **DNA fingerprinting**: Individual identification

### RNA Analysis
- **Northern blotting**: Detect specific RNA molecules
- **RT-PCR**: Amplify RNA via reverse transcription
- **In situ hybridization**: Localize RNA in tissues
- **RNA interference**: Knockdown gene expression

### Protein Analysis
- **Western blotting**: Detect specific proteins
- **Immunoprecipitation**: Isolate protein complexes
- **Mass spectrometry**: Protein identification and quantification
- **X-ray crystallography**: Determine protein structure

## Key Points

- Central dogma describes flow of genetic information from DNA to protein
- DNA replication is semiconservative and requires multiple enzymes
- Transcription produces RNA copies of genetic information
- Translation converts mRNA sequences into protein sequences
- Gene regulation occurs at multiple levels: transcriptional, post-transcriptional, and epigenetic
- DNA repair mechanisms maintain genome integrity
- Cell cycle checkpoints ensure proper DNA replication and division
- Molecular biology techniques enable diagnosis and treatment of genetic diseases
- Understanding molecular mechanisms is crucial for developing targeted therapies
- Advances in genome editing and personalized medicine depend on molecular biology principles
