# Growth and Development

## Overview

Growth and development are fundamental aspects of pediatric medicine that distinguish children from adults. Growth refers to the quantitative changes in body size and composition, while development encompasses the qualitative changes in function and capabilities. Understanding normal patterns of growth and development is essential for identifying abnormalities, providing anticipatory guidance, and supporting optimal child health outcomes.

**Key Concept**: Children are constantly changing beings whose physical, cognitive, social, and emotional capabilities evolve in predictable patterns while maintaining individual variation.

### Importance in Pediatric Care
- **Health surveillance**: Regular monitoring identifies problems early
- **Anticipatory guidance**: Counseling parents about upcoming milestones
- **Early intervention**: Identifying delays allows for timely therapy
- **Parental reassurance**: Understanding normal variation reduces anxiety
- **Prognostic value**: Growth and development predict future health

### Domains of Development
- **Physical growth**: Height, weight, head circumference, body composition
- **Motor development**: Gross motor and fine motor skills
- **Language development**: Receptive and expressive language
- **Cognitive development**: Thinking, reasoning, problem-solving
- **Social-emotional development**: Relationships, emotional regulation
- **Adaptive development**: Self-care and daily living skills

## Principles of Growth and Development

### General Principles

#### Direction of Development
- **Cephalocaudal**: Head to toe (head control before walking)
- **Proximodistal**: Center to periphery (trunk before fingers)
- **General to specific**: Gross movements before fine movements

#### Patterns of Development
- **Sequential**: Skills build upon previous achievements
- **Predictable**: General patterns are consistent across children
- **Individual variation**: Timing varies but sequence is consistent
- **Continuous**: Ongoing process throughout childhood
- **Critical periods**: Sensitive times for specific skill acquisition

#### Influences on Development
- **Genetic factors**: Inherited potential and predispositions
- **Environmental factors**: Nutrition, stimulation, experiences
- **Health status**: Chronic illness, medications, hospitalizations
- **Social determinants**: Family structure, socioeconomic status, culture
- **Biological factors**: Prematurity, birth complications, congenital conditions

### Theoretical Frameworks

#### Piaget's Cognitive Development Theory
- **Sensorimotor (0-2 years)**: Learning through senses and motor activity
- **Preoperational (2-7 years)**: Symbolic thinking, egocentrism
- **Concrete operational (7-11 years)**: Logical thinking about concrete events
- **Formal operational (11+ years)**: Abstract thinking, hypothetical reasoning

#### Erikson's Psychosocial Development Theory
- **Trust vs. Mistrust (0-1 year)**: Developing trust in caregivers
- **Autonomy vs. Shame (1-3 years)**: Independence and self-control
- **Initiative vs. Guilt (3-6 years)**: Taking initiative, purposeful behavior
- **Industry vs. Inferiority (6-12 years)**: Competence and achievement
- **Identity vs. Role Confusion (12-18 years)**: Sense of self and identity

#### Freud's Psychosexual Development Theory
- **Oral stage (0-1 year)**: Pleasure through oral activities
- **Anal stage (1-3 years)**: Control and toilet training
- **Phallic stage (3-6 years)**: Gender identity formation
- **Latency stage (6-12 years)**: Social and intellectual skills
- **Genital stage (12+ years)**: Sexual maturation and intimacy

## Physical Growth

### Growth Parameters

#### Length/Height
- **Measurement techniques**: Recumbent length (<2 years), standing height (≥2 years)
- **Birth**: Average 50 cm (19.7 inches)
- **First year**: Grows 25 cm (10 inches)
- **Second year**: Grows 12 cm (5 inches)
- **Preschool**: 6-8 cm/year (2-3 inches/year)
- **School-age**: 5-6 cm/year (2-2.5 inches/year)
- **Puberty**: Growth spurt 8-12 cm/year (3-5 inches/year)

#### Weight
- **Birth**: Average 3.5 kg (7.7 lbs)
- **First 3 months**: Gains 25-30 g/day (1 oz/day)
- **Doubles birth weight**: By 4-6 months
- **Triples birth weight**: By 12 months
- **Quadruples birth weight**: By 24 months
- **Preschool**: Gains 2 kg/year (4-5 lbs/year)
- **School-age**: Gains 3-3.5 kg/year (6-7 lbs/year)
- **Puberty**: Rapid weight gain with growth spurt

#### Head Circumference
- **Birth**: Average 35 cm (13.8 inches)
- **First 3 months**: Grows 2 cm/month
- **3-6 months**: Grows 1 cm/month
- **6-12 months**: Grows 0.5 cm/month
- **Second year**: Grows 2 cm total
- **Clinical significance**: Reflects brain growth, identifies hydrocephalus or microcephaly

#### Body Mass Index (BMI)
- **Calculation**: Weight (kg) / Height (m)²
- **Age-specific**: Must use BMI-for-age percentiles
- **Underweight**: <5th percentile
- **Healthy weight**: 5th to <85th percentile
- **Overweight**: 85th to <95th percentile
- **Obese**: ≥95th percentile

### Growth Charts

#### Types of Charts
- **WHO growth standards**: Birth to 24 months (breastfed reference)
- **CDC growth charts**: 2-20 years (US population reference)
- **Specialized charts**: Premature infants, Down syndrome, Turner syndrome

#### Interpretation
- **Percentiles**: Position relative to reference population
- **Growth velocity**: Rate of growth over time
- **Channel consistency**: Following same percentile curve
- **Crossing percentiles**: May indicate problem if >2 major lines
- **Secular trends**: Population changes over time

#### Clinical Application
- **Regular plotting**: At all health supervision visits
- **Pattern recognition**: Identify growth abnormalities
- **Parental height**: Mid-parental height calculation
- **Growth prediction**: Target height ranges
- **Nutritional assessment**: Weight-for-height relationships

### Body Composition Changes

#### Proportions
- **Newborn**: Head is 25% of total body length
- **Adult**: Head is 12% of total body length
- **Trunk to leg ratio**: Changes throughout childhood
- **Body fat**: Peaks at 6 months, decreases until age 6, then increases

#### Organ System Growth
- **Neural tissue**: 90% adult size by age 6
- **Lymphoid tissue**: Peaks at 10-12 years, then decreases
- **Reproductive organs**: Minimal growth until puberty
- **General body tissues**: Steady growth throughout childhood

## Motor Development

### Gross Motor Development

#### Newborn to 3 Months
- **Primitive reflexes**: Moro, rooting, sucking, grasp
- **Head control**: Lifting head when prone
- **Positioning**: Flexed posture gradually extends
- **Movements**: Random movements of arms and legs

#### 4-6 Months
- **Head control**: Steady head control achieved
- **Rolling**: Rolls front to back, then back to front
- **Sitting**: Sits with support, tripod sitting
- **Reaching**: Reaching for objects

#### 7-9 Months
- **Sitting**: Sits without support
- **Crawling**: Begins crawling or scooting
- **Pulling to stand**: Uses furniture for support
- **Cruising**: Walking while holding furniture

#### 10-12 Months
- **Standing**: Stands independently
- **Walking**: First independent steps
- **Climbing**: Attempts stairs with assistance
- **Balance**: Improving balance and coordination

#### 13-18 Months
- **Walking**: Walks well independently
- **Running**: Begins to run stiffly
- **Stairs**: Walks up stairs with assistance
- **Ball skills**: Kicks ball forward

#### 18-24 Months
- **Running**: Runs well
- **Stairs**: Walks up and down stairs holding rail
- **Jumping**: Jumps in place
- **Ball skills**: Throws ball overhand

#### 2-3 Years
- **Running**: Runs smoothly with good coordination
- **Stairs**: Alternates feet going up stairs
- **Jumping**: Jumps forward, hops on one foot
- **Tricycle**: Pedals tricycle
- **Ball skills**: Catches large ball, kicks ball well

#### 3-4 Years
- **Balance**: Stands on one foot 4-5 seconds
- **Hopping**: Hops on one foot
- **Stairs**: Alternates feet going up and down
- **Bicycle**: Rides tricycle well

#### 4-5 Years
- **Balance**: Stands on one foot 10 seconds
- **Skipping**: Skips smoothly
- **Jumping**: Broad jump, hop 10+ times
- **Ball skills**: Catches ball with hands

#### 5-6 Years
- **Coordination**: Smooth, coordinated movements
- **Bicycle**: Rides bicycle with training wheels
- **Skating**: May begin skating or swimming
- **Sports**: Can participate in organized sports

### Fine Motor Development

#### 0-3 Months
- **Grasp reflex**: Involuntary grasp
- **Hand regard**: Watches own hands
- **Batting**: Swipes at objects

#### 4-6 Months
- **Reaching**: Purposeful reaching and grasping
- **Raking grasp**: Uses whole hand to grab objects
- **Hand-to-mouth**: Brings objects to mouth
- **Transfer**: Transfers objects hand to hand

#### 7-9 Months
- **Pincer grasp**: Crude pincer (thumb and finger)
- **Banging**: Bangs objects together
- **Poking**: Uses index finger to poke
- **Release**: Voluntary release of objects

#### 10-12 Months
- **Fine pincer**: Refined pincer grasp
- **Pointing**: Points with index finger
- **Container play**: Puts objects in containers
- **Scribbling**: Marks with crayon

#### 12-18 Months
- **Stacking**: Stacks 2-3 blocks
- **Scribbling**: Spontaneous scribbling
- **Turning pages**: Multiple pages at once
- **Drinking**: Uses cup with spillage

#### 18-24 Months
- **Stacking**: Stacks 4-6 blocks
- **Drawing**: Imitates vertical line
- **Turning pages**: One page at a time
- **Spoon use**: Uses spoon with some spillage

#### 2-3 Years
- **Stacking**: Stacks 8+ blocks
- **Drawing**: Copies circle, imitates cross
- **Cutting**: Begins using scissors
- **Self-feeding**: Feeds self well

#### 3-4 Years
- **Drawing**: Copies cross, draws person with 3 parts
- **Cutting**: Cuts along line
- **Dressing**: Buttons large buttons
- **Handedness**: Hand preference established

#### 4-5 Years
- **Drawing**: Copies square, draws person with 6 parts
- **Writing**: Prints some letters
- **Cutting**: Cuts shapes
- **Dressing**: Dresses self completely

#### 5-6 Years
- **Drawing**: Copies triangle, draws detailed pictures
- **Writing**: Prints name, most letters
- **Tying**: May tie shoelaces
- **Utensil use**: Uses fork and knife

## Language Development

### Receptive Language (Understanding)

#### 0-6 Months
- **Startle response**: Reacts to loud sounds
- **Recognition**: Recognizes parent's voice
- **Localization**: Turns to sound source
- **Response**: Responds to name by 6 months

#### 6-12 Months
- **Commands**: Responds to simple commands ("no," "bye-bye")
- **Words**: Understands several words
- **Gestures**: Responds to gestures
- **Recognition**: Recognizes names of familiar objects

#### 12-18 Months
- **Body parts**: Points to body parts when named
- **Objects**: Points to familiar objects
- **Commands**: Follows simple one-step commands
- **Vocabulary**: Understands 50+ words

#### 18-24 Months
- **Two-step commands**: Follows simple two-step commands
- **Vocabulary**: Understands 200+ words
- **Questions**: Responds to simple questions
- **Prepositions**: Understands in, on, under

#### 2-3 Years
- **Complex commands**: Follows two-step unrelated commands
- **Concepts**: Understands size, color, quantity concepts
- **Stories**: Listens to short stories
- **Vocabulary**: Understands 500-900 words

#### 3-4 Years
- **Conversations**: Understands most conversation
- **Questions**: Comprehends who, what, where, why questions
- **Stories**: Follows plot of story
- **Vocabulary**: Understands 1,200-2,000 words

### Expressive Language (Production)

#### 0-6 Months
- **Crying**: Differentiated crying patterns
- **Cooing**: Vowel sounds (2-3 months)
- **Babbling**: Consonant-vowel combinations (6 months)
- **Social sounds**: Laughs, squeals

#### 6-12 Months
- **Babbling**: Varied babbling, jargoning
- **Imitation**: Imitates sounds
- **First words**: 1-2 words by 12 months (mama, dada)
- **Gestures**: Waves bye-bye, shakes head no

#### 12-18 Months
- **Vocabulary**: 5-20 words by 18 months
- **Jargoning**: Unintelligible sentences with inflection
- **Naming**: Names familiar objects
- **Gestures**: Points to desired objects

#### 18-24 Months
- **Vocabulary explosion**: 50+ words, learns new words daily
- **Two-word phrases**: "More juice," "Daddy go"
- **Pronouns**: Uses "me" and "mine"
- **Questions**: Asks "what's that?"

#### 2-3 Years
- **Vocabulary**: 200-1,000 words
- **Sentences**: 3-4 word sentences
- **Plurals**: Uses plurals and past tense
- **Intelligibility**: 50-75% intelligible to strangers

#### 3-4 Years
- **Vocabulary**: 1,000-1,500 words
- **Sentences**: Complete sentences, tells stories
- **Grammar**: Uses most grammatical forms correctly
- **Intelligibility**: 75-100% intelligible to strangers
- **Questions**: Asks many questions (why, how)

#### 4-5 Years
- **Vocabulary**: 1,500-2,500 words
- **Complex sentences**: Uses conjunctions
- **Narratives**: Tells detailed stories
- **Articulation**: Most sounds correct except r, th

#### 5-6 Years
- **Vocabulary**: 2,500+ words
- **Language structure**: Adult-like grammar
- **Reading readiness**: Recognizes letters, rhyming
- **Articulation**: All sounds should be correct by age 8

### Red Flags for Language Delay
- **No babbling by 12 months**
- **No words by 16 months**
- **No two-word phrases by 24 months**
- **Loss of previously acquired skills**
- **Poor intelligibility after age 3**
- **Stuttering that persists or worsens**

## Cognitive Development

### Infancy (0-2 years) - Sensorimotor Stage
- **Reflexive behavior**: Birth to 1 month
- **Primary circular reactions**: 1-4 months (repetitive actions)
- **Secondary circular reactions**: 4-8 months (intentional actions)
- **Coordination of reactions**: 8-12 months (means-end behavior)
- **Tertiary circular reactions**: 12-18 months (trial and error)
- **Mental representation**: 18-24 months (object permanence complete)

### Early Childhood (2-7 years) - Preoperational Stage
- **Symbolic thinking**: Use of symbols, pretend play
- **Egocentrism**: Difficulty seeing others' perspectives
- **Centration**: Focus on one aspect of situation
- **Irreversibility**: Cannot mentally reverse actions
- **Animism**: Attributing life to inanimate objects
- **Magical thinking**: Cause and effect misunderstandings

### Middle Childhood (7-11 years) - Concrete Operational Stage
- **Conservation**: Quantity remains same despite appearance changes
- **Classification**: Organizing objects into categories
- **Seriation**: Ordering objects along dimension
- **Reversibility**: Mental reversal of operations
- **Decentration**: Consider multiple aspects simultaneously
- **Concrete logic**: Logical thinking about concrete situations

### Adolescence (11+ years) - Formal Operational Stage
- **Abstract thinking**: Reasoning about hypothetical situations
- **Hypothetico-deductive reasoning**: Systematic problem-solving
- **Metacognition**: Thinking about thinking
- **Idealism**: Imagining ideal situations
- **Future orientation**: Long-term planning and goals

## Social-Emotional Development

### Attachment (0-2 years)
- **Attachment formation**: Strong bond with primary caregivers
- **Stranger anxiety**: 6-12 months
- **Separation anxiety**: 8-18 months (peaks at 14-18 months)
- **Secure base**: Using caregiver as base for exploration
- **Social referencing**: Looking to caregiver for cues

### Autonomy (1-3 years)
- **Independence**: "Me do it" attitude
- **Negativism**: "No" is favorite word
- **Temper tantrums**: Expression of frustration
- **Parallel play**: Playing alongside but not with peers
- **Emotional regulation**: Learning to manage emotions

### Initiative (3-6 years)
- **Pretend play**: Elaborate imaginative play
- **Cooperative play**: Playing with peers, sharing
- **Gender identity**: Understanding gender roles
- **Emotional expression**: Broader range of emotions
- **Empathy**: Beginning to understand others' feelings

### Industry (6-12 years)
- **School achievement**: Focus on competence and skills
- **Peer relationships**: Increasing importance of friendships
- **Self-esteem**: Based on accomplishments and peer acceptance
- **Rule-governed behavior**: Understanding and following rules
- **Moral development**: Sense of right and wrong

### Identity Formation (12-18 years)
- **Identity exploration**: "Who am I?" questions
- **Peer influence**: Strong peer group identification
- **Independence**: Separating from parents
- **Self-consciousness**: Heightened awareness of self
- **Values formation**: Developing personal value system

## Developmental Screening

### Purpose and Principles
- **Early identification**: Detect delays before obvious impairment
- **Universal screening**: All children at regular intervals
- **Standardized tools**: Validated screening instruments
- **Surveillance plus screening**: Combination approach most effective
- **Referral for evaluation**: Screen-positive requires comprehensive assessment

### Recommended Screening Schedule (AAP)
- **9 months**: Developmental and autism screening
- **18 months**: Developmental and autism screening
- **24 months**: Developmental and autism screening
- **30 months**: Developmental screening
- **Annual**: School-age developmental/behavioral surveillance

### Developmental Screening Tools

#### Ages and Stages Questionnaire (ASQ-3)
- **Age range**: 1-66 months
- **Domains**: Communication, gross motor, fine motor, problem-solving, personal-social
- **Format**: Parent-completed questionnaire
- **Administration time**: 10-15 minutes

#### Parents' Evaluation of Developmental Status (PEDS)
- **Age range**: Birth to 8 years
- **Approach**: Elicits parental concerns
- **Format**: 10 questions
- **Advantages**: Quick, focuses on parent observations

#### Denver Developmental Screening Test II (Denver II)
- **Age range**: Birth to 6 years
- **Domains**: Personal-social, fine motor-adaptive, language, gross motor
- **Format**: Direct observation
- **Administration time**: 20 minutes

### Autism Screening Tools

#### Modified Checklist for Autism in Toddlers (M-CHAT-R/F)
- **Age range**: 16-30 months
- **Format**: Parent questionnaire with follow-up
- **Items**: 20 yes/no questions
- **Follow-up**: Structured interview for positive screens

#### Autism Spectrum Screening Questionnaire (ASSQ)
- **Age range**: 6-17 years
- **Format**: Parent or teacher questionnaire
- **Items**: 27-item checklist

### Response to Screening Results

#### Screen-Negative
- **Continue surveillance**: Monitor at subsequent visits
- **Anticipatory guidance**: Age-appropriate counseling
- **Rescreening**: At next recommended interval

#### Screen-Positive
- **Developmental evaluation**: Comprehensive assessment
- **Early intervention referral**: Age 0-3 years (Part C)
- **School evaluation**: Age 3+ years (Part B)
- **Medical evaluation**: Rule out medical causes
- **Parent support**: Resources and education

## Growth Disorders

### Short Stature

#### Definition
- **<3rd percentile**: Height below 3rd percentile for age and sex
- **>2 SD below mean**: More than 2 standard deviations below mean
- **Growth velocity**: Crossing percentiles downward
- **Below mid-parental height**: Height potential not achieved

#### Evaluation
- **History**: Prenatal, birth, development, nutrition, family history
- **Physical examination**: Dysmorphic features, proportions, Tanner staging
- **Growth pattern**: Review growth charts for pattern
- **Bone age**: X-ray of left hand and wrist
- **Laboratory studies**: CBC, metabolic panel, thyroid function, IGF-1, karyotype

#### Causes

##### Constitutional Growth Delay
- **Pattern**: Delayed bone age, delayed puberty
- **Family history**: Often positive for late bloomers
- **Prognosis**: Normal adult height achieved
- **Management**: Reassurance, monitoring

##### Familial Short Stature
- **Pattern**: Bone age matches chronological age
- **Family history**: Short parents
- **Prognosis**: Short adult height consistent with genetics
- **Management**: Reassurance

##### Pathologic Causes
- **Endocrine**: Growth hormone deficiency, hypothyroidism
- **Genetic**: Turner syndrome, Noonan syndrome
- **Skeletal dysplasias**: Achondroplasia
- **Chronic disease**: Inflammatory bowel disease, renal disease
- **Malnutrition**: Inadequate caloric intake
- **Intrauterine growth restriction**: Small for gestational age

### Tall Stature

#### Definition
- **>97th percentile**: Height above 97th percentile
- **Growth velocity**: Accelerated growth rate
- **Above mid-parental height**: Exceeds genetic potential

#### Causes
- **Familial tall stature**: Tall parents
- **Constitutional advancement**: Early maturation
- **Endocrine**: Precocious puberty, hyperthyroidism
- **Genetic syndromes**: Klinefelter syndrome, Marfan syndrome
- **Excess growth hormone**: Gigantism (rare)

### Failure to Thrive (FTT)

#### Definition
- **Weight <3rd percentile**: Persistently low weight
- **Crossing percentiles**: Falling >2 major percentile lines
- **Weight for length**: <5th percentile

#### Classification
- **Inadequate caloric intake**: Most common cause
- **Malabsorption**: Celiac disease, cystic fibrosis
- **Increased caloric needs**: Hyperthyroidism, chronic infections
- **Ineffective use**: Metabolic disorders

#### Evaluation
- **Detailed history**: Feeding history, diet recall, family/social history
- **Physical examination**: Signs of neglect, abuse, organic disease
- **Observation**: Feeding observation, parent-child interaction
- **Laboratory studies**: Based on clinical findings

#### Management
- **Nutritional intervention**: Increase caloric density
- **Feeding strategies**: Structured feeding times
- **Social support**: WIC, food assistance, parenting support
- **Monitoring**: Frequent weight checks
- **Address underlying cause**: Treat organic causes

### Obesity

#### Definition (BMI-for-age)
- **Overweight**: 85th to <95th percentile
- **Obesity**: ≥95th percentile
- **Severe obesity**: ≥120% of 95th percentile or BMI ≥35

#### Complications
- **Metabolic**: Type 2 diabetes, metabolic syndrome
- **Cardiovascular**: Hypertension, dyslipidemia
- **Orthopedic**: Slipped capital femoral epiphysis, Blount disease
- **Psychosocial**: Depression, low self-esteem, bullying
- **Other**: Sleep apnea, NAFLD, PCOS

#### Evaluation
- **History**: Diet, activity, screen time, family history
- **Physical examination**: BMI, blood pressure, acanthosis nigricans
- **Laboratory studies**: Fasting glucose, lipid panel, liver enzymes

#### Management
- **Lifestyle modification**: Primary treatment approach
- **Dietary changes**: Healthy eating patterns, portion control
- **Physical activity**: 60 minutes daily moderate-vigorous activity
- **Screen time reduction**: <2 hours daily recreational screen time
- **Family involvement**: Family-based approach most effective
- **Behavioral interventions**: Goal-setting, self-monitoring
- **Pharmacotherapy**: Consider for severe obesity in adolescents
- **Bariatric surgery**: Rarely, for severe obesity with comorbidities

## Developmental Delays and Disabilities

### Global Developmental Delay
- **Definition**: Delay in 2+ developmental domains
- **Severity**: Mild, moderate, severe, profound
- **Causes**: Genetic, metabolic, structural brain abnormalities
- **Evaluation**: Comprehensive developmental assessment

### Intellectual Disability
- **Definition**: IQ <70 plus adaptive functioning deficits
- **Onset**: During developmental period
- **Severity levels**: Mild (IQ 50-70), moderate (35-50), severe (20-35), profound (<20)
- **Management**: Educational interventions, adaptive skills training

### Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
- **Prevalence**: 1 in 36 children (CDC 2023)
- **Core features**: Social communication deficits, restricted/repetitive behaviors
- **Red flags**: No babbling by 12m, no gestures by 12m, no words by 16m, loss of skills
- **Diagnosis**: Clinical diagnosis using DSM-5 criteria
- **Management**: Early intensive behavioral intervention, speech therapy, OT

### Cerebral Palsy
- **Definition**: Non-progressive motor disorder from brain injury
- **Types**: Spastic, dyskinetic, ataxic, mixed
- **Risk factors**: Prematurity, perinatal asphyxia, infections
- **Associated conditions**: Intellectual disability, epilepsy, vision/hearing problems
- **Management**: Multidisciplinary approach, PT, OT, orthopedic care

### Learning Disabilities
- **Reading disorder**: Dyslexia - difficulty with reading accuracy/fluency
- **Mathematics disorder**: Dyscalculia - difficulty with math concepts
- **Written expression disorder**: Dysgraphia - difficulty with writing
- **Diagnosis**: Psychoeducational testing
- **Management**: Educational accommodations, specialized instruction

### Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)
- **Prevalence**: 8-10% of school-age children
- **Symptoms**: Inattention, hyperactivity, impulsivity
- **Diagnosis**: Clinical criteria, rating scales, school observation
- **Management**: Behavioral interventions, stimulant medications
- **Comorbidities**: Learning disabilities, anxiety, oppositional defiant disorder

## Early Intervention and Special Services

### Early Intervention (Birth to 3 years)
- **Federal program**: Part C of IDEA
- **Eligibility**: Developmental delay or diagnosed condition
- **Services**: PT, OT, speech therapy, special instruction
- **Setting**: Natural environments (home, daycare)
- **Family-centered**: IFSP (Individualized Family Service Plan)

### Special Education (Ages 3-21)
- **Federal program**: Part B of IDEA
- **Eligibility**: Specific disability categories affecting education
- **Services**: Special education, related services
- **Setting**: Least restrictive environment
- **Planning**: IEP (Individualized Education Program)

### Medical Home Coordination
- **Comprehensive care**: Coordinate all aspects of child's health
- **Care coordination**: Link medical and educational services
- **Family support**: Resources and advocacy
- **Transition planning**: Prepare for adulthood

## Anticipatory Guidance by Age

### Newborn to 2 Months
- **Feeding**: Breastfeeding support, formula feeding
- **Sleep**: Back to sleep, safe sleep environment
- **Safety**: Car seat, never shake baby
- **Development**: Tummy time, responsive caregiving

### 2-6 Months
- **Feeding**: Introduction of solids at 4-6 months
- **Sleep**: Establishing bedtime routine
- **Safety**: Childproofing, supervision
- **Development**: Language exposure, social interaction

### 6-12 Months
- **Feeding**: Self-feeding, cup introduction
- **Sleep**: Sleep training if needed
- **Safety**: Gates, outlet covers, choking hazards
- **Development**: Reading books, responsive play

### 12-24 Months
- **Feeding**: Table foods, family meals
- **Sleep**: Transitioning to one nap
- **Safety**: Water safety, poisoning prevention
- **Development**: Language stimulation, limit setting

### 2-5 Years
- **Nutrition**: Balanced diet, limit juice and sweets
- **Sleep**: Consistent bedtime routine
- **Safety**: Street safety, stranger danger
- **Development**: Preschool readiness, social skills

### 5-12 Years
- **Nutrition**: Healthy eating habits
- **Activity**: Sports participation, limit screen time
- **Safety**: Bicycle helmets, water safety
- **Development**: School performance, peer relationships

### Adolescence
- **Nutrition**: Healthy weight management
- **Activity**: Regular physical activity
- **Safety**: Driving safety, substance use prevention
- **Development**: Identity formation, future planning

## Key Points

- Growth and development are continuous processes with predictable patterns and individual variation
- Regular monitoring using growth charts and developmental screening tools is essential
- Developmental domains include physical, motor, language, cognitive, and social-emotional development
- Developmental theories (Piaget, Erikson, Freud) provide frameworks for understanding child development
- Growth disorders include short stature, tall stature, failure to thrive, and obesity
- Developmental delays require early identification and intervention for optimal outcomes
- Autism screening should occur at 18 and 24 months per AAP guidelines
- Early intervention services are available for children birth to 3 years with delays
- Anticipatory guidance tailored to age helps parents support optimal development
- Family-centered care and parental partnership are essential for supporting child development
