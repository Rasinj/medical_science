# Radiology

## Directory Overview

This directory contains comprehensive educational content on clinical radiology, covering essential imaging modalities, interpretation principles, and key findings across major body systems. The focus is on practical application for clinicians ordering and interpreting imaging studies.

## Learning Objectives

After studying this directory, learners should be able to:

1. **Chest Imaging**
   - Systematically interpret chest X-rays
   - Recognize common thoracic pathologies
   - Understand indications for CT chest
   - Identify pneumonia, pneumothorax, pleural effusions, masses

2. **Abdominal Imaging**
   - Choose appropriate imaging for clinical scenarios
   - Recognize acute abdominal conditions
   - Interpret common findings on CT abdomen
   - Understand bowel obstruction, appendicitis, cholecystitis patterns

3. **Neuroimaging**
   - Differentiate CT vs MRI indications
   - Recognize acute stroke on imaging
   - Identify traumatic brain injuries
   - Understand spine imaging for common pathologies

4. **Musculoskeletal Imaging**
   - Interpret plain radiographs for fractures
   - Understand MRI for ligament and tendon injuries
   - Recognize arthritis patterns
   - Identify bone tumors and infections

5. **Interventional Radiology**
   - Understand common IR procedures
   - Know indications for IR interventions
   - Recognize complications
   - Appreciate IR role in multidisciplinary care

## Imaging Modalities Covered

- **Plain Radiography (X-ray)**: Chest, abdomen, skeletal
- **Computed Tomography (CT)**: All body regions
- **Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)**: Neurologic, MSK focus
- **Ultrasound**: Abdominal, pelvic, vascular
- **Fluoroscopy**: GI studies, procedures
- **Nuclear Medicine**: Basic concepts
- **Interventional Techniques**: Image-guided procedures

## Systematic Interpretation Emphasized

Each file teaches systematic approach:
1. Confirm patient identification and exam
2. Assess technical adequacy
3. Systematic review of all structures
4. Identify abnormalities
5. Formulate differential diagnosis
6. Correlate with clinical presentation
7. Recommend further imaging if needed
