# Systemic Pathology

## Overview

Systemic pathology is the study of disease processes organized by organ systems, applying fundamental pathological principles to specific anatomical structures. This approach integrates general pathology concepts with clinical medicine, providing a systematic understanding of how diseases affect individual organ systems and their interrelationships.

**Content Status**: Comprehensive organ system-specific pathology coverage
**Quality Features**: Detailed pathophysiology, clinical correlations, diagnostic criteria
**Last Updated**: January 2025

## Conceptual Framework

### Integration with General Pathology
- **Cellular and tissue injury**: Applied to specific organ contexts
- **Inflammation**: Organ-specific inflammatory patterns and diseases
- **Neoplasia**: Site-specific tumor biology and behavior
- **Hemodynamic disorders**: System-specific vascular pathology
- **Genetic and metabolic diseases**: Organ manifestations of systemic disorders

### Clinical Pathology Correlation
- **Morphological diagnosis**: Gross and microscopic pathology
- **Laboratory findings**: Biochemical and immunological markers
- **Imaging correlation**: Radiological-pathological relationships

- **Prognosis and treatment**: Pathology-guided therapeutic approaches

## Study Approach

### Systematic Learning Strategy
1. **Review general pathology**: Ensure understanding of fundamental principles

3. **Compare and contrast**: Identify similarities and differences between diseases

## Disease Classification Systems

### Etiological Classification
- **Genetic**: Inherited, chromosomal, multifactorial
- **Infectious**: Bacterial, viral, fungal, parasitic
- **Immunological**: Autoimmune, hypersensitivity, immunodeficiency
- **Neoplastic**: Benign, malignant, precancerous
- **Metabolic**: Acquired, inherited metabolic disorders
- **Degenerative**: Age-related, wear-and-tear
- **Iatrogenic**: Drug-induced, procedure-related
- **Environmental**: Toxins, radiation, physical injury

### Pathophysiological Classification
- **Inflammatory**: Acute, chronic, granulomatous
- **Proliferative**: Hyperplasia, neoplasia
- **Degenerative**: Atrophy, degeneration
- **Vascular**: Ischemic, hemorrhagic, thrombotic
- **Obstructive**: Stenosis, occlusion, compression
- **Functional**: Metabolic, endocrine, neurological

### Morphological Classification
- **Gross pathology**: Macroscopic appearance, size, shape, color
- **Microscopic pathology**: Histological patterns, cellular changes
- **Ultrastructural**: Electron microscopy findings
- **Immunohistochemical**: Protein expression patterns
- **Molecular**: Genetic and molecular alterations

## Diagnostic Methods

### Histopathological Techniques
- **Tissue processing**: Fixation, embedding, sectioning, staining
- **Special stains**: Histochemical stains for specific tissue components
- **Immunohistochemistry**: Antibody-based protein detection
- **In situ hybridization**: RNA and DNA detection in tissues
- **Electron microscopy**: Ultrastructural examination

### Molecular Pathology
- **Genetic testing**: Mutation analysis, chromosomal studies
- **Gene expression profiling**: mRNA expression patterns
- **Next-generation sequencing**: Comprehensive genomic analysis
- **PCR techniques**: Amplification of specific sequences
- **FISH**: Fluorescence in situ hybridization for chromosomal abnormalities

### Clinical Laboratory Integration
- **Biochemical markers**: Enzymes, proteins, metabolites
- **Immunological tests**: Antibodies, immune complexes, complement
- **Microbiological studies**: Culture, serology, molecular diagnostics
- **Hematological analysis**: Blood counts, morphology, coagulation
- **Cytology**: Examination of cells from fluids and aspirates
