# Gynecology Surgery

## Overview

Gynecologic surgery encompasses operative procedures for conditions affecting the female reproductive system, including benign diseases and malignancies. The specialty has evolved significantly with the introduction of minimally invasive techniques, including laparoscopy, robotic surgery, and vaginal approaches. Modern gynecologic surgery emphasizes fertility preservation when appropriate, minimally invasive approaches, and multidisciplinary care for complex conditions.

## Surgical Principles

### Minimally Invasive Surgery
- **Laparoscopy**: Conventional and advanced procedures
- **Robotic Surgery**: Enhanced visualization and dexterity
- **Hysteroscopy**: Intrauterine pathology
- **Vaginal Surgery**: Natural orifice approach

### Oncologic Surgery
- **Staging**: Complete surgical staging for malignancies
- **Cytoreduction**: Maximal tumor removal
- **Lymphadenectomy**: Pelvic and para-aortic nodes
- **Fertility preservation**: When appropriate

### Reconstructive Surgery
- **Pelvic floor repair**: Restore anatomy and function
- **Mesh augmentation**: Select cases
- **Autologous repair**: Primary approach

## Perioperative Care

### Enhanced Recovery Protocols
- Multimodal analgesia
- Early feeding and mobilization
- Minimize tubes and drains
- Thromboprophylaxis
- Antimicrobial prophylaxis

### Complication Prevention
- Surgical site infection prevention
- Venous thromboembolism prophylaxis
- Urinary tract injury prevention
- Bowel injury prevention
- Nerve injury avoidance

## Key Points

- Gynecologic surgery ranges from office procedures to complex oncologic operations
- Minimally invasive approaches reduce recovery time and complications
- Oncologic surgery requires specialized training and staging procedures
- Pelvic reconstructive surgery restores anatomy and quality of life
- Obstetric surgery addresses pregnancy-related complications
- Fertility preservation is priority when appropriate
- Enhanced recovery protocols improve outcomes
- Multidisciplinary care essential for complex cases
